Course Organization

The class website is the central location for information about Bi 1x. Contact information for the course instructors and TAs may be found here, as well as a schedule of all activities. Each activity has a link to an associated document. For experiments, the document provides background on the experiment, protocols, and assignments. Documents for tutorials and journal clubs are also linked to from the syllabus page.

Attendence

Bi 1x is focused on projects that depend on protocols carried out during class time. Thus, attendance is 100% mandatory. Tardiness is unacceptable and will affect your grade. Anyone that misses more than 2 class meetings without a formal excuse from a dean or health care provider will be automatically dismissed from the course.

Journal Club

During the course, we will read various articles and discuss them in class. The journal club articles may be downloaded from the class website, along with talking points to get you thinking about pertinent questions ahead of the in-class discussion. You should have answers ready ahead of time from the talking points to share with the class.

Assignments

Assignments are found in the master document for each of the experiments in the course. They are designed to help step you through the data analysis and to help you understand the experiment in greater depth. They consist of n + 1 problems pertaining to the respective lab module. Problems 0 and n are always the same. Problem 0 asks you to write a summary of the lab module. Problem *n* asks you to show any data or code. Problems 1 through *n* help take you through the data analysis and ask you to think about conclusions.

Attention:

Some problems are prelab questions. They are labeled as such with blue text. These are best done before lab because they will help you better comprehend what you are doing during the experiment. There is no "going through the motions" in Bi 1x!

Assignments are due **before class** on the prescribed due date. They must be submitted electronically by emailing them to ``bi1x@caltech.edu``. Only PDF documents will be accepted (no Worddocuments). If you have handwritten mathematics in your assignment, you may include scans in your submission, but everything must be legibile. The name of the document must be ``lastname_firstname_#.pdf``, where ``#`` is the number of the module as shown on the schedule page.

Lab Notebooks

Keeping an up-to-date and accurate record of your work in lab is always important. You must therefore keep a neat and thorough lab notebook. An example set up for a lab notebook entry is shown below.

Already in notebook when you arrive at lab:

  • Date
  • Title of experiment
  • Purpose of experiment in one or two sentences (What you are trying to find out and how you plan to do that).
  • Methods: Bullet-point outline of what you will do in lab. Note that copy-and-pasting of the handouts into the lab notebook is not acceptable.

    • To be filled in during lab:

  • Comments on methods: Mark out any deviations from the protocols you used in the lab.
  • Observations and data: write down any observations as well as any associated data. Clearly write out any calculations you did in lab. It is important to keep it organized. For example, you could make a table that lists the amounts of solutions you used for each reaction. This is separate from data analysis activities, which are turned in separately as part of the assignment.
  • Any notes you think might be important for future reference.

    • Please save the first two pages of your lab notebook for a table of contents page and list all of your experiments and their page number after every class.

    Grading

    The grade breakdown is as follows:

  • Lab performance: 15%
  • Lab notebook: 10%
  • Assignments: 65%
  • Journal club: 10%

    • Lab Performance

    Your lab performance is based on attendance, attitude, and and how carefully you work. First and foremost, you must work safely. You are also expected to take care with all equipment and to keep it clean. Your lab area must also be clean and tidy.

    Lab Notebook

    Your notebooks will be checked in class for completeness and turned in later to be graded for accuracy. Half of the credit is given for prelab on experimental information including the purpose and methods. (This is different from the prelab questions in the assignments, which count toward the assignments grade.) The other half of the credit for lab notebooks is for in-lab notes including comments on procedures, observations, data and results. Be sure your lab notebook is legible and well-organized.

    Assignments

    The assignments are due at the beginning of class on the due date specified on the class website. The exceptions are the prelab questions for the assignment, which must be handed in before the experiment is performed in the designated lab period. Assignments may be hand-written, but must be legible. Obviously, and plots of data must be generated by computer. Late assignments without a formal excuse from a dean or health care provider will have 10% credit deducted for each day that it is late (in other words, you can get up to 90% credit if one day late, up to 50% if five days late, and so on). You are encouraged to collaborate on the assignments, whether organizing ideas or analyzing data. However, you must turn in your own data, your own solutions to the problems, and your own code. When writing code, the 50 foot rule applies. For experiments in groups, you can share data but you must write your own analysis.

    Journal Club

    You will receive credit for contributions to journal club discussions. Therefore, you should come to journal club prepared to discuss the talking points.
